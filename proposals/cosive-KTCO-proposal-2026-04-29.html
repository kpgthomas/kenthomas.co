Executive Summary

The Problem

Ten years of building a name in cyber threat intelligence has put Cosive two degrees from the entire AU/NZ industry, with a 36% conversion rate and 8-year customer relationships. The way you sell lives in the founders' heads. Everyone who sells learned it on the job. To get past the $3M ceiling and on to the $5M gate where dedicated hires make sense, the way Cosive sells needs to be documented, replicable, and run day-to-day by someone other than Terry and Chris.

Our Solution

Phase 1 (Extract): codify how Cosive already sells into a Revenue Architecture Playbook tailored to your two motions, APAC consulting and global CloudMISP.

Phase 2 (Embed): Pete Solway, Founder Sales Solution, inside the business as your dedicated revenue leader: hour-a-day discipline, sales-marketing handoff, live deal coaching.

Phase 3 (Evolve): your team running it without us in the room.

The Investment

Phase 1: $10,000 + GST. $5,000 on signing, $5,000 on completion.

Phase 2: $1,000 to $5,000 + GST per month, calibrated to support needs. $250 + GST per hour with success bonus.

Phase 3: included in Phase 2.

The Outcome

Cosive crosses $3M annualised revenue within 12 months of Phase 2 commencement, with the structure in place to bring on a dedicated salesperson, customer success manager, and developer at the $5M gate.

Phase Details

We'll build your revenue system in phases, proving each step works before moving to the next.

Phase 1

Extract: Revenue Architecture Playbook (4-5 weeks)

What we'll do:

  • Nine one-hour sessions and five 30-minute sessions over 4 to 5 weeks with Terry: we extract how Cosive sells today, then validate and refine until the playbook reads like you wrote it yourself
  • Refined ICPs and competitive landscape for both motions: APAC consulting (CTISO, security operations, software dev) and global CloudMISP (CTI hubs, banks, regulated organisations starting their threat-intel journey)
  • Two distinct buying journey maps: the long, relational consulting cycle and the faster, marketing-led CloudMISP cycle. One playbook, two pathways, no compression
  • Sales-marketing handoff designed with Tash and the Backstory ABM workflow so the pipeline Tash builds matches the one the sales team works
  • Customer expansion framework for the relationships that already last 8 years and 18+ projects deep, so the next CSM hire walks into a documented motion at the $5M gate
  • MEDDIC qualification structure built into Pipedrive deal stages with disqualification criteria, so the team stops using gut feel on qualify-out
  • Permission-to-market outreach sequences for a CISO audience that is the most-targeted person in their building. Value-first, white-paper-led, no ambulance-chasing
  • Pipedrive automation and Make.com workflow specifications: sequences, follow-ups, reporting hooks, all owned and edited by Cosive in Confluence

What you'll get:

A comprehensive 14-part Revenue Architecture Playbook, codified in your Confluence wiki, integrated with Pipedrive and Make.com. The most complete revenue document Cosive has ever had. You own the IP outright. If we never work together again, you still have a system your team can follow.

Phase 2

Embed: Implementation (6-24 months)

What we'll do:

  • Pete embedded inside Cosive as your dedicated revenue leader: the single point of contact for everything revenue, including managing the daily cadence and the weekly rhythm
  • Weekly one-on-ones with each team member doing sales: real deal coaching using MEDDIC against live opportunities for Terry, Chris, Prescott, Ash, and Nisu
  • Hour-a-day sales discipline enforced across the team. Not aspirational. Actual
  • Facilitation of the weekly sales/marketing sync with Tash, with clear handoffs and a shared target list driven by Backstory and ABM experiments
  • Outreach experiment design and results review, so the team stops re-running the same A/B tests on six-week cycles
  • Monthly playbook iteration based on what's working in the field, kept current in Confluence
  • Pipeline review and forecasting in Pipedrive. Visibility for the leadership team. Quarterly review against the $3M target

What you'll get:

A team executing the playbook without Terry or Chris in every deal. Pipeline generated through a documented account-based process, not founder hustle. The hour-a-day rhythm holding. The CloudMISP product line growing predictably alongside consulting.

Phase 3

Evolve: Revenue Independence

What we'll do:

  • Support hiring the dedicated salesperson at the $5M gate, including referral to Pointer (KTCO's recruitment partner) for sourcing against the playbook
  • Onboarding playbook applied to the new hire so they're closing deals against documented frameworks from week one, not building from scratch
  • Customer success manager hire scoped against the existing 8-year customer relationships and the expansion framework already in place
  • Step-back transition: KTCO and FSS reduce involvement as the internal team takes ownership. Quarterly check-ins, not weekly

What you'll get:

Revenue independence. Cosive sells without us in the room, with a dedicated salesperson and CSM running against the playbook. The system that got you to $3M is the same system that takes you to $5M and beyond.

Why We Will Deliver

☰ Our Approach

Cosive didn't reach $2.5M and 36% conversion by accident. The way you sell already works. KTCO doesn't come in and tell you what to do. 80 to 90% of the playbook IS how Cosive already sells, codified, with frameworks and structure layered on top. The remaining 10% is the gold from working across more than 10 bespoke playbooks: what's actually a known-good principle versus what's marketing folklore. Pete then runs it. The most common reason a playbook fails is that nobody owns it. Pete is the owner.

The Founder Sales Solution scorecard you and Chris completed last week confirmed what you already know: the conversion engine is strong, the lead generation and the structured handoff between marketing and sales are where the gaps sit. The playbook addresses both directly.

  • Client in the GRC space: we helped them flip their partner model. The playbook codified the new motion so the partner channel could be run consistently without the founder in every conversation. Same business, repositioned around how the market actually wanted to buy.
  • Founder-led B2B accounting services: a founder hired someone whose previous job was selling beer to start in their first sales role. The new hire closed 18 deals in 10 weeks because the playbook removed the variable. The business went from $100K months to half-a-million-dollar months. Same playbook, different industry; the principles transfer because they're first principles, not tactics.
  • Outsourcing company: we built a webinar straight off the back of the playbook. One asset, structured around the buying journey rather than a feature pitch, generated 40 sales-accepted leads.

The last hire didn't work out: the playbook removes the variable. The process exists, documented and tested, before any new person joins. When the right person follows the process, the result is the result.

The 2-3 month cash hump before CloudMISP renewals: Phase 1 is fixed at $10K, paid $5K up front and $5K on completion. Phase 2 commences 22 June, aligned with CloudMISP renewal cash flow. We don't soften the offer; we time it to your cycle.

The trusted-advisor brand can't be hurt by aggressive selling: no 2-2-1 outreach, no volume cold spam. The playbook leads with value, permission-to-market sequencing, white-paper-first contact. Built around the reality that CISOs are the most-targeted person in their building.

We only win when you win: the success bonus is tied to Cosive crossing $3M. If the playbook doesn't get implemented and the milestone doesn't hit, we don't get paid for the upside. That's deliberate.

Our Assumptions

For this to work, we're assuming:

About Your Business

  • Cosive operates as a dual-motion business: APAC consulting (long, relational, in-region) and global CloudMISP (faster, product-led, marketing-fed)
  • Pipedrive remains the system of record. Confluence remains the wiki home of the playbook. Make.com remains the integration layer between them and Jira
  • The team of technologists doing sales has the appetite to follow a documented process, not just intuition. Today's behaviour confirms this
  • The new website launches on schedule (~2-3 weeks from today) with the LLM-friendly, ICP-targeted positioning Tash is building

About Your Market

  • The CISO buyer in AU/NZ stays saturated with vendor pitches; differentiation comes from value-first, trusted-advisor positioning, not from outbound volume
  • European banks and Middle Eastern hubs continue to push regulated threat-intel sharing (NIS2, fraud data) which expands the CloudMISP addressable market
  • The Google AI summary effect on inbound is structural, not temporary. Outbound and ABM need to fill the gap deliberately
  • Direct competitors in the CloudMISP space ("box-in-the-cloud" providers) don't move into Cosive's managed-service-plus-consulting positioning during the engagement

About Working Together

  • Terry is the primary point of contact during Phase 1, with approximately 12 hours of face time across the 4-5 week build
  • Chris is consulted for delivery-side and product-side context but not blocked daily; his time goes to consulting delivery
  • Tash is included in the relevant sessions on the sales-marketing handoff and the ABM playbook
  • Cosive completes the action items between sessions on the agreed cadence; the playbook is owned and edited by Cosive in Confluence once delivered

About Success

  • Success is Cosive crossing $3M annualised revenue within 12 months of Phase 2 commencement
  • Measurement is the trailing 4-quarter revenue figure as recorded in Cosive's accounts; agreed on a quarterly review cadence with Pete
  • The 12-month timeline reflects sales cycles of 6 weeks to a year in the consulting motion; the CloudMISP renewal cycle is the leading indicator
  • ROI is the gap between current revenue and $3M, multiplied by GP, against the total Phase 1 + Phase 2 + outcome payment investment over 12 months
