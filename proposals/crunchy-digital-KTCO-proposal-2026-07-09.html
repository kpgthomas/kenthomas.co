What we found:

  • Brand guidelines are documented and used, and you know your ideal client and your point of difference.
  • Almost none of it is written down, so it cannot be handed to anyone else.
  • No CRM, and no documented vision to anchor the team.

Methods

71%

What this means: Whether you have a defined, teachable way to sell or if it's still instinct and improvisation.

What we found:

  • Three live lead channels, documented discovery questions, and strong, tailored proposals and case studies.
  • Lead qualification is informal, in your head, and prospects go quiet in the pipeline.
  • No system for growing accounts or generating referrals from your existing clients.

Metrics

47%

What this means: Whether you're tracking the right numbers to know what's working and what isn't.

  • You track where every lead comes from and tag them by source.

  • No reliable forecast, and no early warning on client churn.

Bottom Line

Your methods are strong; the gaps are documentation and measurement, not knowledge. Get how you win out of your head, put a CRM and a few numbers underneath it, and build a client and referral rhythm, and the business stops depending on you being in every deal. We start by mapping your sales engine. After that, you choose one of two ways to put it in place: hands-on with Tammie, or a lighter coaching pack. Nothing after the mapping is locked in until you have the plan in front of you.

Sales Engine Strategy

  • Help you define the role, hire and embed someone to close the bigger construction and industrial clients
